TIME TRAVELLER'S GUIDE

Captain Ray "Time Jumper" Johnson (Visited: Cyberpunk Metropolis, 2142)

Amazing trip! Got a cybernetic arm upgrade for free—until I realized the warranty didn’t cover 'time travelers with outdated currency.' Now I’m stuck with a glowing arm and no charger. 4/5 stars.

Lady Eleanor Whitmore (Visited: The Roaring Twenties, 1925)

What a marvelous era! Accidentally introduced selfies to a group of flappers, and now they keep demanding ‘pocket paintings’ before every dance. Hope I didn’t break history. 5/5 stars!

Megan Thompson (Visited: The Salem Witch Trials, 1692)

DO NOT bring a smartphone to 1692. DO NOT use the flashlight function in front of Puritans. I don’t want to talk about what happened next. Negative stars if possible.

Private First Class Tony "Boomerang" Carter (Visited: Wild West, 1875)

I came for the saloon duels, but they challenged me to a poker game instead. I lost all my credits and had to work as a blacksmith for three weeks. Horseshoes are harder to make than they look. 3/5 stars.

Professor Linda Hartwell (Visited: Victorian London, 1888)

Loved the tea culture, hated the corsets. Also, someone mistook my smartwatch for a 'wizard’s amulet,' and now Sherlock Holmes is suspicious of me. 5/5 stars for mystery lovers!

Doug McAllister (Visited: Jurassic Safari, 66 Million BCE)

Wanted to see a T-Rex up close—turns out, I was on the menu! Ran for my life, lost a shoe, and invented the first-ever 'running sneakers.' Would do it again (with better insurance). 3.5/5 stars.
